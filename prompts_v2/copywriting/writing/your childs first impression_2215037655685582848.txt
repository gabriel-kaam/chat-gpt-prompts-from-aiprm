Not every child discovers their true potential. Many go through their early years without knowing: Who am I? What makes me unique?

The book "Your Child's First Impression" takes you on a journey of discovery:

When does your child begin to develop self-awareness?

How can you nurture their cognitive abilities from birth?

And what is your role as a mother or father in providing the right guidance?

Because your child holds a treasure within them… don't leave them to discover it alone.

PromptHint: One day, I sat watching a three-year-old boy standing in front of a mirror. He touched his nose, then the nose of the image in front of him. He laughed, then pointed his little finger at himself, saying, "That's me." It wasn't just a passing childish remark; it was the moment of his awakening. The moment he realized for the first time that there was an "I" separate from the world, from his mother, and from everyone around him. That small moment is the essence of this book. Every child is born with a hidden treasure: immense mental abilities, unique personality traits, and intellectual potential waiting to be discovered. But the problem isn't the existence of the treasure, but rather finding someone to teach the child the keys to unlocking it. Many children grow up, passing through their formative years, without truly knowing: Who am I? What makes me unique? What are my true feelings? And how do I understand the world around me? This book isn't just a theoretical guide to mental awareness. It's a journey of discovery that begins in early childhood and extends through every stage of life, where we learn together how to build in our children the ability to understand themselves first, and then to understand others and the world. You will find answers to crucial questions: When does a child truly begin to discover themselves? (And we won't just tell you the month and year, but the signs you can see with your own eyes.) How can you gently nurture your child's cognitive development, without pressure, comparison, or misguided direction? And what is your true role—as a parent, teacher, or caregiver—in being a guide, not a commanding figure? In these pages, I have compiled the essence of what psychology and neurodevelopmental sciences have learned, blended with real stories, practical activities, and clear steps. The goal is not for your child to become a genius in the traditional sense, but rather a self-aware individual, capable of confidently saying in the future: "I know who I am, and I know what I want." Let's start together from the beginning. From the first look in the mirror, to the first feeling of distinction, to the first responsible decision. Because your child is waiting for you to help them paint their first face... on the mirror of their soul. This book is your gift to your child before it is their gift to themselves.

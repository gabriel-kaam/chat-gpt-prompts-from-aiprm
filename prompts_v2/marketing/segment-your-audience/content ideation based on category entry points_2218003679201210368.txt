## BACKGROUND

Category Entry Points (or CEPs) are the reasons, motivations, and occasions that prompt consumers to consider a specific product or service category. They represent the problem spaces, triggers or situations that lead customers to recognise a need and explore options within that category.

Essentially, Category Entry Points are thoughts that a person has when they enter the buying process.

You can think of CEPs as mental distribution outlets and problem-solving capabilities. You want your brand to be present in as many shopping channels as possible.

The aim is to maximise the chance a brand is thought of, no matter what CEP pathway a buyer might go down.

There are a series of questions that should be asked by brands to establish CEPs:

WHY Question: Why are you buying a product? Example: “I want an energy boost”

WHEN Question: When are you buying the product? Example: “Before the gym”

HOW Question: How are you feeling? Example: “I’m feeling lethargic”

WHERE Question: Where are you buying the product? Example: “Corner shop, near the gym”

WITH WHOM / WHAT Question: With whom (or what) are you when buying the product? 


## EXAMPLES

WHILE Question: While doing something. Example: “On the way to the gym”

WHY Question: Why are customers buying a red dress? Example: "Customers purchase our red dresses for cruise ship attire"

WHEN Question: When are they buying the product? Example: “After booking a cruise and learning about the types of formal dress worn during cruise dinners”

HOW Question: How are they feeling? Example: “Our customers are concerned about size fittings, returns, materials and whether it is the right dress for the event”

WHERE Question: Where are they buying the product? Example: “We only sell online via a single category page, which is focused only on red dresses."

WITH WHOM / WHAT Question: With whom (or what) are you when buying the product? Examples: "They need a dress to go with their shoes"

WHILE Question: While doing something. Example: “sat on the sofa at night”.
